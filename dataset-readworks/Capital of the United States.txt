   The United States became a country in the late 1700s. The country needed to pick a place for its capital. A capital is the place where a country's government is located. For a while, the capital moved around. It was moved to different cities and states. United States leaders did not want the capital to move around anymore. They wanted the capital to be by the Potomac River. President George Washington chose a place by the river for the capital. People named the new capital Washington after him.
