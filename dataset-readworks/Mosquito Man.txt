 a portrait of Dr. Carlos Juan FinlayMany years ago, people were getting sick with fever.  Some sick people had a yellow color in their eyes or on their skin. The sickness was called “yellow fever.” No one could tell what caused it.A doctor named Carlos Juan Finlay lived in Cuba.  People there were getting the fever. Dr. Finlay studied the disease. He thought that mosquito bites might be causing it. He told other doctors his idea. They did not believe him. They called him “mosquito man.”Years later, scientists tested Dr. Finlay’s idea. They found that he was right. Mosquitoes were the cause! People could stay safe from yellow fever by keeping mosquitoes away.Today, people can receive a vaccine to stay safe from yellow fever.
