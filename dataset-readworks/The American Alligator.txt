   The American alligator is a reptile. A reptile is an animal with scales on its skin. It is cold-blooded. That means it needs the sun to keep warm. American alligators live in the southeastern part of the United States. They live near fresh water. They can swim. They also walk on land. These alligators are large. They can grow up to fifteen feet long. Some may weigh a thousand pounds! American alligators eat fish, turtles, and birds. They eat other small animals too. American alligators lay eggs. Alligator babies hatch from the eggs. The newly hatched babies are six to eight inches long.
