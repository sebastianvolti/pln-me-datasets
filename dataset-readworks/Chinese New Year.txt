Â Red lanterns put up for Chinese New Year Chinese New Year is a big holiday in China. It is also celebrated by some people in the United States. The date of this holiday changes every year. It is always in the winter, though. It happens sometime between January 21 and February 20. People spend the holiday with their family. They have a big dinner the night before the New Year starts. They eat fish, noodles, and more. The noodles are a sign of long life. People also wear red around this time. Red is thought to bring good luck.
